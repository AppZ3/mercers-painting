What roof restoration covers

Roof restoration isn't just painting. A proper restoration is a system: clean, repair, seal, coat. Skip any step and the finish fails early. We do the full job, not a quick paint over.

Tile roofs (concrete & terracotta)

High-pressure clean to remove moss, lichen and chalking. Replace cracked tiles. Re-bed and re-point ridges with flexible pointing compound. Prime with the right base, then membrane and topcoat.

Metal & Colorbond roofs

Treat any rust spots back to bare metal. Etch primer on bare steel. Two coats of metal-grade roof paint colour-matched to original or a fresh new colour. Restores reflectivity and prevents further corrosion.

Re-bedding & re-pointing

Old mortar bedding cracks and lets water in. We strip back the old pointing, lay a fresh bedding compound where needed, then apply flexible polymer pointing in a colour-matched finish.

Broken tile replacement

Cracked or missing tiles get matched and replaced before painting. We carry common Bristile, Monier and CSR replacement stock or source as needed.

Valley & flashing checks

While we're on the roof we check valleys, flashings around chimneys and skylights and gutter brackets. Anything obvious gets flagged or fixed before paint.

Heat-reflective coatings

Optional heat-reflective topcoats reduce roof surface temperatures by 15 to 30°C. Worth considering if your upstairs rooms are hard to cool in summer.

Our process, start to finish

A roof restoration done right takes 4 to 7 days depending on size. Anyone offering it in two is skipping steps. Here's what we actually do.

  • On-site inspection. Free, walked-and-quoted. We check tile condition, bedding, valleys, flashings, gutters. You get a proper written quote, not a back-of-envelope number.
  • High-pressure cleaning. Mould, moss, lichen, chalking. Pressure washed off so we have a clean substrate to coat. Drains and gutters protected.
  • Repair phase. Cracked tiles replaced. Bedding mortar re-laid where needed. Ridge capping re-pointed with flexible compound. Rust spots treated on metal.
  • Primer / sealer. The right primer for the substrate. Tile sealer for porous tiles. Etch primer on bare metal. Skipping this is the #1 reason roof paint fails.
  • Membrane coat. A heavy-bodied membrane coat is rolled on across the roof to build film thickness and waterproof the surface.
  • Topcoat. Two coats of UV-stable roof paint in your chosen colour. Heat-reflective option available.
  • Clean up & warranty. Site cleaned, gutters cleared of overspray. Warranty provided on the workmanship in writing.

Roof restoration FAQ

How often does a roof need restoring?
Tile roofs typically need restoration every 10 to 15 years in coastal Northern Rivers conditions. Metal and Colorbond roofs hold up longer (15 to 25 years) but lose colour and develop rust spots that should be treated before they spread.
Can you change the colour of my roof?
Yes. Roof restoration is a chance to change the look of your home. We can colour-match to a paint chart, your gutters, or a sample. Bring photos of homes you like the look of and we'll match.
How long does a roof restoration take?
Most homes are 4 to 7 working days, weather permitting. We need dry conditions for the membrane and topcoat phases. We give you a realistic schedule and update you if weather changes the plan.
Will it add value to my home?
A restored roof significantly improves kerb appeal and pre-sale value, especially on older homes. Real estate agents in the Northern Rivers regularly recommend roof restorations before listing.
Do you provide a warranty?
Yes. We provide written workmanship and product warranties on completion. Product warranty depends on the coating chosen but is typically 10 years on the membrane system.
