What our interior painting covers

Interior work is where the difference between a careful painter and a fast one shows up most. Sharp cut-in lines, smooth ceilings, no overspray on hardware and finishes that don't telegraph every roller stroke six months later.

Full room repaints

Walls, ceiling, cornices, skirting, architraves, doors. We move and protect your furniture. Drop sheets on floors. Hand-cut around fittings.

Whole-house interiors

Room-by-room schedule that lets you keep living in the house. We work clean and contain dust during sanding. Mostly two coats, sometimes three on darker change-overs.

Ceilings

Flat-finish ceiling paint, hand-cut at cornices, rolled and back-rolled for even coverage. We fix any patching the plasterer missed before the topcoat goes on.

Feature walls

Single accent walls, two-tone schemes, painted panelling, half walls. We help with colour matching to your furniture, joinery and existing trim.

Doors, trim & architraves

Doors taken off if needed, sanded, primed where required, finished in gloss or semi-gloss enamel. Crisp lines, no brush marks.

Kitchen & bathroom touch-ups

Repaints around cabinetry, splashbacks and high-moisture areas using mould-resistant finishes. Suitable prep for steamy rooms.

Our process, start to finish

Interior repaints look simple from the outside. The reason some last and others don't is in the small things: how the patches are sanded, whether the primer was correct for the substrate, how clean the cut-in lines are. We do the small things properly.

  • On-site consultation. Free quote with a walk-through. We talk colours, finishes, scope, timing and how you want to live around the work.
  • Colour consultation. Included. We bring sample pots, look at them in your natural light, talk through ceiling and trim coordination.
  • Preparation. Move and cover furniture. Drop sheets on floors. Sand existing surfaces, fill nail holes and cracks, patch where needed, dust everything down.
  • Priming. Stain-blocking primer over patches or smoke-damaged surfaces. Sealer on bare plaster. We don't skip primers just because the original colour is similar.
  • Two top coats. Premium low-sheen acrylic on most walls. Brushed and rolled, never sprayed inside unless specifically requested. Two coats minimum, sometimes three.
  • Clean up. Drop sheets folded, dust vacuumed, furniture replaced, walkthrough with you before we leave.

Interior painting FAQ

How often should I repaint the interior of my home?
High-traffic areas (hallways, kitchens, kids' rooms) typically need a refresh every 5 to 7 years. Bedrooms, lounges and ceilings can stretch to 8 to 12 years. Quality of the original paint job and natural light exposure both shorten or extend that.
Do I need to move out during the work?
No. We work room-by-room and clean up at the end of each day. Most clients live in the house through the job. We can sequence rooms to keep your kitchen and main bedroom available throughout.
Can you do low-VOC or eco-friendly paints?
Yes. Most major brands now have low-VOC interior ranges. We can also do plant-based and mineral paints if needed. Mention it during the quote so we can price properly.
How long does a typical interior repaint take?
A single room is usually 1 to 2 days. A three-bedroom house is 1 to 2 weeks. Whole-house repaints with detailed trim work can run 2 to 3 weeks. We give you a realistic schedule before starting.
Do you do feature walls and two-tone schemes?
Yes, often. We can help with colour selection and trim coordination. We bring sample pots so you can see colours in your actual room light before committing.
