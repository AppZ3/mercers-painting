What our exterior painting covers

Coastal Northern Rivers homes take a beating from salt, sun and humidity. Paint that survives Sydney suburbia won't survive a Byron summer. We choose products and techniques specifically for this climate, then we install them with the prep work that makes them last.

Weatherboard repaints

Pressure clean, sand, fill, prime any bare timber, two top coats. Replace any rotted boards before paint. Cut-in at trims and architraves done by hand.

Render & brick

High-pressure wash to remove salt and chalking. Patch render cracks. Acrylic membrane primer, then two coats of low-sheen exterior paint built for masonry.

Fibre cement & cladding

Joints checked, gaps sealed, faces sanded. Specialist primer that bonds to Hardie-style boards. Two coats of exterior acrylic with UV-stable pigments.

Eaves, fascia & gutters

Often the first thing to fail on a coastal home. We scrape, treat any rust on metal gutters, prime with the right base for the substrate, then finish in colour-matched gloss or low-sheen.

Outdoor timber & decks

Decks, posts, pergolas, fences, screens. Strip back, sand to bare timber where needed, apply oil-based stain or paint depending on look. Maintenance schedules so you don't have to redo it in two years.

Doors & windows

Front doors brushed for that proper hand-finished look. Window frames cut in clean. We mask glass and hardware properly so there's no overspray to scrape off.

Our process, start to finish

The reason exterior paint jobs fail early is rarely the paint. It's the preparation. We invest the time up front so the finish lasts five to ten years instead of two.

  • On-site consultation. Free quote, walked around the property with you. We talk through what you want, what your house actually needs and any priority areas.
  • Colour consultation. Included free. We bring sample pots, look at them in your light, talk through trim and accent colours.
  • Preparation. Pressure wash, scrape, sand, fill, replace rotted timber, treat any rust, mask glass and hardware. This is where most exterior jobs are won or lost.
  • Priming. The right primer for each substrate. Bare timber gets a different primer than chalky render or galvanised metal. We don't skip steps.
  • Two top coats. Premium acrylic, always two coats minimum. Brushed and rolled by hand on detail areas, airless sprayed on large flat sections for an even film build.
  • Clean up & walkthrough. Site cleaned, paths swept, drop sheets out. We walk you around the finished job to make sure you're happy before we leave.

Exterior painting FAQ

How often should I repaint the exterior of my Northern Rivers home?
Most homes here need a full exterior repaint every 7 to 10 years. North and west facing walls fail first. Coastal homes within 1km of the ocean often need touch-ups every 4 to 5 years on the most exposed elevations.
Can you paint over render that's cracked or chalking?
Yes, but the cracks need filling first and chalking surfaces need a binding primer or they'll fail again within months. We assess each elevation during the quote and price the preparation properly.
Do you use low-VOC or eco-friendly paints?
We can. Most major brands now have low-VOC exterior ranges that perform comparably to standard formulations. If that's a priority for you, mention it when we quote.
How long does a typical exterior repaint take?
A standard three-bedroom home takes 5 to 8 working days depending on size, preparation needed and weather. Larger or two-storey homes can run 2 to 3 weeks. We give you a realistic timeline upfront.
Are you insured?
Yes. Mercer's Precision Painting carries public liability and workers compensation cover. We're OH&S compliant and happy to provide certificates on request before work starts.
